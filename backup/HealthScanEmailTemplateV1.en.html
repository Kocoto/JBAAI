Heart Rate Variability (HRV) & Autonomic Nervous System
Mean RRi [Mean RRi VALUE] ms Higher at rest Average time between consecutive heartbeats. Longer interval corresponds to lower heart rate and higher parasympathetic (PNS) activity.
RMSSD [RMSSD VALUE] ms Higher is generally better Root mean square of successive RR interval differences. Reflects beat-to-beat variability, related to parasympathetic (PNS - rest, recovery) activity.
SDNN [SDNN VALUE] ms > 50 ms (varies by age/gender) Standard deviation of all normal RR intervals. Reflects overall heart rate variability (HRV). Higher HRV often indicates better fitness and stress resilience.
SD1 [SD1 VALUE] ms Higher is generally better Poincaré plot standard deviation perpendicular to the line of identity. Related to short-term HRV, primarily reflecting parasympathetic (PNS) activity.
SD2 [SD2 VALUE] ms Consult Doctor Poincaré plot standard deviation along the line of identity. Related to long-term HRV, reflecting both sympathetic (SNS) and parasympathetic (PNS) activity.
LF/HF [LF/HF VALUE] 0.27 - 0.38 (Per PDF)
(Consult Further) 		Ratio of Low Frequency (LF) to High Frequency (HF) power bands of HRV. Reflects autonomic balance. Higher ratio indicates increased sympathetic (stress) activity.
