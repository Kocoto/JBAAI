Basic Vitals
Metric Value Desired (Reference) Explanation
Blood Pressure (mmHg) [Blood Pressure VALUE] <129/90 Blood Pressure – This refers to the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries. It is recorded with two numbers: the systolic pressure (the first number) measures the pressure when the heart beats, while the diastolic pressure (the second number) reflects the pressure when the heart rests between beats.
Heart Rate [Pulse Rate VALUE] 60–100 bpm Healthy adults normal resting heart rate is between 60-100 bpm. Heart rate is the number of times your heart beats per minute. This number fluctuates as you go through your day.
Oxygen Saturation (%) [Oxygen Saturation VALUE] >95% Oxygen Saturation, or SpO2, is a measure of how much oxygen the red blood cells are carrying from the lungs to the rest of the body. Normal SpO2 for healthy lungs ranges between 95%-100%..

PRQ [PRQ VALUE] ~5 The PRQ reflects the efficiency with which the heart and lungs are working together.

Risk Assessment
Metric Value Desired (Reference) Explanation
High Blood Pressure Risk [High Blood Pressure Risk VALUE] Low High Blood Pressure Risk – This result indicates whether your blood pressure exceeds the predefined systolic and diastolic thresholds. The risk levels are categorized as Low, Medium, or High. A higher result means your blood pressure is above the recommended limits, potentially indicating an increased risk of hypertension.
High Hemoglobin A1c Risk [High HbA1c Risk VALUE] Low High Hemoglobin A1c Risk – This result shows whether your Hemoglobin A1c level exceeds a predefined threshold. The possible risk levels are Low, Medium, or High. A higher result indicates that your HbA1c level is above the recommended limit, which may suggest an increased risk of poor blood sugar control.
Low Hemoglobin Risk [Low Hemoglobin Risk VALUE] Low Low Hemoglobin Risk indicates if your hemoglobin level is below the healthy threshold, which may signal anemia. Hemoglobin is a protein in red blood cells that transports oxygen throughout the body.
  • Possible results: Low or High
  • A high result means hemoglobin is below the normal level
  • Low levels reduce oxygen delivery, causing fatigue, dizziness, or shortness of breath
  • Monitoring helps detect early signs of anemia and supports better energy and overall health
Heart Age [Heart Age VALUE] Same as or less than real age Framingham Heart Age estimates the biological age of your heart by comparing your cardiovascular risk factors to those of an ideal, healthy individual of the same sex. It helps communicate heart health in an easy-to-understand way.
  • Based on the Framingham Heart Study
  • Compares your risk to that of someone with optimal health (no smoking, normal blood pressure, healthy cholesterol)
  • Heart age reflects the age of a healthy person with the same risk level as you
Interpretation:
  • Heart age = actual age: Cardiovascular health is average
  • Heart age < actual age: Better-than-average heart health
  • Heart age > actual age: Higher cardiovascular risk; consider lifestyle changes
ASCVD Risk (%) [ASCVD Risk VALUE] <30% ASCVD (Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease) Risk estimates the likelihood of experiencing an atherosclerotic cardiovascular event within 10 years. The ASCVD score is calculated using our SDK's vital signs data combined with user demographic information, including:
  • Age – Within the Framingham-supported range
  • Sex – Male/Female
  • BMI (Height and weight)
  • Smoker – Yes/No
This score strongly correlates with the Framingham ASCVD score for predicting 10-year risk. The ASCVD risk score will fall within one of the following categories:
  • Low: Below 1%
  • Moderate: Between 1%–30%
  • High: Above 30%
High Total Cholesterol [High Total Cholesterol Risk VALUE] Low High Total Cholesterol Risk – Indicates if your total cholesterol level is above the recommended threshold. Higher levels may increase the risk of artery narrowing (atherosclerosis) and cardiovascular disease. Since high cholesterol often has no symptoms, regular monitoring helps guide lifestyle changes to protect heart health.
HRV & Autonomic Nervous System
Metric Value Desired (Reference) Explanation
HRV SDNN (ms) [SDNN VALUE] ≥50 ms SDNN is a calculated parameter of Heart Rate Variability (HRV) that quantifies the variability of the time intervals between consecutive heartbeats. HRV SDNN is used in various stress related vitals.
Mean RRi (ms) [Mean RRi VALUE] Higher at rest Mean RRi is the average time between RR intervals (RRi), the intervals between successive heartbeats. A longer Mean RRi indicates a lower heart rate and higher parasympathetic cardiac activation.
LF/HF Ratio [LF/HF VALUE] 0.27–0.38 LF/HF Ratio – The ratio of Low Frequency (LF) to High Frequency (HF) components in Heart Rate Variability (HRV), reflecting the balance of the autonomic nervous system. A higher ratio suggests increased sympathetic (stress-related) activity, while a lower ratio indicates parasympathetic (rest-related) dominance.
Stress Assessment
Metric Value Desired (Reference) Explanation
Stress Level [Stress Level VALUE] Low/Normal Stress Level – Indicates how stressed your body is, based on Heart Rate Variability (HRV). Mild stress can help you stay alert and focused, but prolonged stress may affect your health. This index is for reference only and should be monitored regularly.
Stress Index [Stress Index VALUE] Lower is better Stress Index – A numerical value derived from Heart Rate Variability (HRV) that reflects your body’s current stress state. Based on Baevsky’s method, it indicates how active your autonomic nervous system is. A higher index means higher physiological stress.
